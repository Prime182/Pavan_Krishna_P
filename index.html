Data Visualization Intern — Indian Meteorological Department, RMC, Chennai

May 2024 – Jun 2024

My internship at the Indian Meteorological Department was a deep dive into climate data, focusing on the Discomfort Index (DI). I undertook a comprehensive analysis of 10 years (2013–2023) of hourly DI data, utilizing Python and Tableau to uncover critical patterns. Through this analysis, I identified significant positive correlations between temperature and DI, alongside varying correlation patterns with humidity, providing a clearer picture of discomfort levels.

I then translated these findings into compelling trend visualizations, highlighting the increasing discomfort during the May–August seasons over the years. This work culminated in proposing advanced forecasting models, including SARIMAX, and public alert systems, complete with interactive dashboards. A key finding from this experience was the critical importance of hourly granularity for precise DI trend analysis, which is vital for public health alerting and proactive measures.
